# Empathy Map — Bookshelve

> Produced by: `facilitating-inception` Phase 2 (ux-agent)

## Persona: The Avid Listener
> Gets 3–5 book recommendations per week from podcasts and friends. Currently saves them in a notes app but never looks at it.

### Says
- "Someone just recommended a great book but I know I'll forget it by tomorrow"
- "I have a list somewhere but I don't know where"
- "I don't want to sign up for another thing"

### Thinks
- My to-read list is embarrassingly long and I feel guilty about it
- I should just pick something and read it

### Does
- Screenshots book covers from Instagram
- Saves links to Amazon but never buys
- Asks friends "what was that book you mentioned?"

### Feels
- Frustrated when a recommendation evaporates
- Mildly anxious about the growing backlog
- Satisfied when they actually finish a book

### Pains
**P1:** Recommendations are lost before they're acted on
**P2:** Existing tools (Goodreads, Notion) feel heavy for such a simple need
**P3:** No clear signal for "what should I read next"

### Gains
**G1:** Instant capture — zero friction between hearing a recommendation and saving it
**G2:** A clear, short list they trust — not a graveyard of old saves
**G3:** The small satisfaction of marking a book as read
